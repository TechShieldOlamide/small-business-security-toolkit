## Why You Need One

If your team is sharing passwords through email, WhatsApp, or a shared document, you have a security problem right now. Anyone who gains access to that email account, chat, or document gets access to everything. A password manager fixes this by giving each person only the access they need, in a way that does not require trusting every message platform you use.

---

## What to Look For

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Shared vaults | Let teams share specific logins without sharing the actual password |
| Role-based access | Each person sees only what their role requires |
| 2FA support | Adds a second layer of protection beyond the master password |
| Cross-platform | Works on phones, laptops, and browsers your team actually uses |
| Easy onboarding | Non-technical staff need to be able to use it without training sessions every time |
| Audit logging | Shows who accessed what and when |
| Reasonable pricing | Small teams should not need enterprise pricing tiers |

## Recommended Options

### Bitwarden
**Best for:** Most small teams
**Why:** Strong encryption, affordable Teams plan, supports shared collections with item-level permissions, works well for non-technical users, open source and well audited.

### 1Password
**Best for:** Teams that want extra polish and customer support
**Why:** Very user friendly interface, strong shared vault features, good mobile app experience. Slightly more expensive than Bitwarden.

### Dashlane
**Best for:** Teams that also want a VPN bundled in
**Why:** Combines password management with a VPN service. Good for businesses that travel frequently or work across networks they do not control.

---

## What to Avoid

- Browser-only password saving (Chrome, Safari) with no central admin control
- Shared spreadsheets or documents listing passwords
- Sending passwords through email or chat apps, even "for now"
- One shared master password for the entire team

---

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] Choose a password manager based on team size and budget
- [ ] Create the team/organisation account
- [ ] Set up individual logins for each team member
- [ ] Create shared vaults or collections based on role
- [ ] Migrate existing passwords into the manager
- [ ] Delete passwords from any insecure locations (documents, emails, chat history)
- [ ] Enable 2FA on the password manager account itself
- [ ] Train the team on basic usage (autofill, sharing, requesting access)
- [ ] Document the setup in plain English for future reference
