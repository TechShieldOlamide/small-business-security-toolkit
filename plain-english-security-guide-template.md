## Why This Matters

We handle [customer data / payment information / business records] and a few simple habits keep that information safe. This guide covers what you need to do, day to day, to keep our business secure.

---

## 1. Passwords

We use [Password Manager Name] to manage all our logins. You should never need to remember a password except the one master password to get into [Password Manager Name].

**What to do:**
- Log into [Password Manager Name] using the credentials provided to you
- Use the autofill feature instead of typing passwords manually
- If you need access to something you do not have, message [Name/Role]
- Never share your password manager login with anyone, including coworkers

---

## 2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

You will notice that logging into [Email/CRM/Banking system] now asks for a code in addition to your password. This is two-factor authentication and it is one of the most important things protecting our business.

**What to do:**
- When asked, open [Authenticator App Name] on your phone and enter the code shown
- If you get a 2FA prompt you did not trigger yourself, tell [Name/Role] immediately. This could mean someone is trying to access your account.

---

## 3. Sharing Information with Contractors or Remote Staff

If you need to give a contractor or virtual assistant access to a tool, do not send them a password directly.

**What to do:**
- Contact [Name/Role] to set up proper access through [Password Manager Name]
- Never send login details through email, WhatsApp, or text message

---

## 4. Email Safety

Most security problems start with email. A few habits prevent most issues.

**What to do:**
- Be cautious of emails asking you to click a link or enter login details, even if they look like they are from someone you know
- If an email feels off, ask [Name/Role] before clicking anything
- Never enter your password manager login details anywhere except the official app or website

---

## 5. When Someone Joins or Leaves the Team

**If someone joins:**
Contact [Name/Role] so they can be set up with proper access from day one.

**If someone leaves:**
Contact [Name/Role] immediately so their access can be removed.

---

## 6. What to Do If Something Feels Wrong

If you suspect your account has been compromised, you clicked something suspicious, or anything about our systems feels off:

1. Tell [Name/Role] immediately, do not wait
2. Do not try to fix it yourself first
3. There is no penalty for raising a false alarm. Raising it late is the real risk.
