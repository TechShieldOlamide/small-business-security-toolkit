# Access Provisioning and Deprovisioning Checklist

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** June 2026  
**Built by:** TrustGrid Technology Limited  

---

## Why This Matters

Most small business security failures do not come from sophisticated hacking. They come from a former employee who still has access to the company email three months after leaving, or a new hire who was given access to everything because nobody took the time to set it up properly.

This checklist gives you a repeatable process for both situations.

---

## When Someone Joins

- [ ] Identify exactly which systems and accounts the new person needs access to
- [ ] Create individual logins for each system (never share an existing login)
- [ ] Set access permissions based on their role, not broader "just in case" access
- [ ] Add them to the relevant password manager vault or collection
- [ ] Enable 2FA on their accounts from day one
- [ ] Provide a brief walkthrough of the password manager and any security tools
- [ ] Document what access was granted and when
- [ ] Set a 90-day reminder to review whether their access level still matches their role

---

## When Someone Changes Roles

- [ ] Review their current access against their previous role
- [ ] Remove access to systems no longer relevant to their new role
- [ ] Add access to systems required by the new role
- [ ] Update their vault or collection assignments in the password manager
- [ ] Document the change with date and reason

---

## When Someone Leaves (Most Critical)

- [ ] Revoke access to all systems immediately upon confirmed departure, ideally before they leave the building or end the call
- [ ] Disable their email account or set up forwarding as needed
- [ ] Remove them from the password manager entirely
- [ ] Change any shared passwords they had direct knowledge of
- [ ] Revoke access to any cloud storage, CRM, or business software
- [ ] Collect any company devices
- [ ] Review recent activity logs for their accounts in the weeks following departure
- [ ] Document the offboarding date and confirm all steps were completed

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Waiting until the next IT review cycle to remove access for someone who already left
- Giving new hires broad access "to make things easier" rather than role-specific access
- Forgetting that access includes not just logins but also things like building access codes, company credit cards, and shared cloud storage
- Not having a written record of who has access to what
